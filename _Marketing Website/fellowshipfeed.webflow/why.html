The Fellowship Feed is an easy to use app designed to provoke intent & empower communication in communities among the disconnected state of the American, Christian-Church.

Guiding Priciples

1.

Church is NOT a building
The Bible clearly shows us that the “Church” is body of believers. This does not refer to a physical building or even a 501c3 organization. Maintaining a close-knit community is essential to being a Christian and completing our task to preach the gospel to all nations. FF is here to boost communication between your church family.

3.

Jesus alone is the head of the Church
The early church... acts 2:42

5.

Not everyone is tech-savy
Using technology can be challenging and frustrating to some. Those who feel comfortable using Fellowship Feed will notice it is specially designed to be easy and straight-forward to use. Most activity takes place on a single page. No profiles or searching necessary. FF is designed for computers, smartphones, and tablets. Posting to your community can be as easy as sending an email or SMS. No fancy-pants tech needed.

2.

Jesus alone is the head of the Church
Fellowship feed will not be replacing the work of the Holy Spirit or the personal discipleship and fellowship of church members. Think of Fellowship Feed as a tool to aid in connection these genuine propellants of the Church.

4.

Technology is here to stay
Technology isn’t a tool to pass aside. It should be harnessed and used by transformed minds for God’s glory. Smartphones, Wifi, tablet PCs, even smart glasses are now embedded into schools, culture, and even the next gen church. Many churches are embracing these new tools. The current and older generations might not care for this new tech, but these next generations come fluent.

6.

Always Free
This service is designed to provoke intent & empower communication in church communities. Could you really put a price on that? Lord willing, this service will not have any ads, fees, and will NOT be sold for a profit. It is free of charge to anyone. Genuine love typically requires a sacrifice (Just like how our savior died for us.). What a great opportunity to share this resource and serve your church community.
